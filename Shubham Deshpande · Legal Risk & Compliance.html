01  ·  Selected Work
Fraud  ·  HDFC Ltd
Spotting Loan Fraud Before It Became a Problem
Part of managing a large loan portfolio is recognising when something doesn't add up. Across the Central Maharashtra region, I identified a pattern of irregularities — fabricated title documents, inconsistent income declarations and suspected identity misrepresentation in several loan files. Because the loans were tagged to my region, it was my responsibility to act.

Rather than push straight for litigation — which would have been slow, public and damaging — I escalated to Head Office in Mumbai, built the evidentiary file and led direct negotiations with the parties involved. The goal was recovery with minimum brand exposure, and that's what we achieved.
Matters settled through structured negotiation — recovery protected, no adverse regulatory attention, no public proceedings.
Merger Compliance  ·  2023
When India's Largest Merger Hit My Desk
In July 2023, HDFC Ltd merged into HDFC Bank — the largest merger in Indian corporate history. For most people it was a headline. For me, it was 800+ active court and tribunal proceedings that all needed updating to reflect the new legal entity.

I was the sole legal resource in Central Maharashtra throughout the transition. Every substitution application at DRT, DRAT and NCLAT, every updated tribunal record, every piece of compliance data sent to Head Office — that was my responsibility. We couldn't afford proceedings to lapse or records to be wrong. None did.
Full legal transition completed across all active files — no proceedings disrupted, all records updated within the merger compliance window.
Litigation Management  ·  DRT · DRAT
800 Cases. One Person. One Region.
The Central Maharashtra zone had over 800 active litigation cases when I was there — across the Debt Recovery Tribunal, DRAT, NCLAT, Arbitration and Consumer Courts. Many involved ancestral loan defaults, where the original borrower had died and legal heirs were disputing enforcement. These cases required careful handling — legally, practically and sometimes personally.

There was no legal team. I briefed external counsel, tracked every hearing, managed evidence files and kept Head Office reporting accurate. It was demanding, and it taught me a level of case ownership and accountability that's difficult to replicate in a larger team setting.
Portfolio managed with full data integrity across the entire tenure — Head Office reporting delivered without interruption.
NCLAT  ·  IBC  ·  Insolvency
Protecting Creditor Position in Insolvency Proceedings
Several corporate borrowers in the portfolio entered insolvency proceedings under the Insolvency & Bankruptcy Code. When matters reached the National Company Law Appellate Tribunal, I assisted external counsel in building HDFC Ltd's case as a secured financial creditor.

This meant pulling together loan statements, security documents, demand notices and correspondence into a coherent evidentiary record — making sure the institution's position was documented, defensible and clearly presented at appellate stage. I coordinated strategy with Head Office and managed the files end to end.
Secured creditor status upheld in all supported matters — creditor position maintained at appellate stage throughout tenure.
AML  ·  SAR  ·  Clyde Solicitors London
Filing a SAR in the Middle of a Live Client Matter
At Clyde Solicitors, I was handling AML due diligence for clients across three jurisdictions — UK, India and East Africa under UK Money Laundering Regulations 2017. Several matters involved multi-layered corporate structures where UBO tracing required working through multiple holding entities.

One client flagged during enhanced screening. I prepared the internal Suspicious Activity Report, escalated to the MLRO under POCA 2002 and managed the process through to resolution — without the client matter being derailed. It required careful judgement about timing, communication and what could and couldn't be disclosed.
Full compliance maintained — matter resolved, SAR process completed correctly, no adverse SRA findings on file review.
KYC  ·  EDD  ·  HDFC Bank
A PEP That Needed More Than a Standard KYC Form
At HDFC Bank post-merger, a prospective client came through with political affiliations that triggered Enhanced Due Diligence under RBI KYC Master Direction 2016 — classifying them as a Politically Exposed Person.

Standard KYC wasn't sufficient. I ran source of funds verification, cross-checked against OFAC and UN sanctions lists, escalated to the branch compliance officer and prepared a full risk assessment file for senior sign-off. The account was approved conditionally — with enhanced monitoring and mandatory quarterly review built into the relationship from day one.
EDD process completed correctly — account approved with appropriate controls, no findings on subsequent RBI audit.
Arbitration  ·  HDFC Ltd
Builder Disputes and Counterclaims — Arbitration in Practice
Not all disputes in the portfolio went to court. Several ended up in arbitration — builder disputes, title challenges and borrower counterclaims against enforcement. These required a different kind of preparation: position papers, contractual documentation, and a clear narrative for the arbitrator.

Where disbursements were disputed, I worked with the technical and credit teams to build a complete evidentiary record — showing clearly what conditions had been met, when security had been created, and why the institution's position was sound.
All arbitration matters concluded with HDFC Ltd's security position intact — no adverse awards in the zone during tenure.
RBI Inspection  ·  KYC Remediation
Getting a Branch Ready for an RBI Inspection
Before an RBI compliance inspection at HDFC Bank, I ran a KYC remediation exercise across the branch — reviewing customer files, identifying gaps, flagging expired records and incomplete CDD profiles. Each file that wasn't right got a remediation action. High-risk accounts went first.

It was methodical, unglamorous work — but it's exactly the kind of preparation that determines whether a compliance function is genuinely embedded or just theoretical. I also prepared the branch compliance summary for the regional compliance officer ahead of the inspection.
Branch received a satisfactory RBI rating — no major findings raised on inspection.
